Blood Tests

Hospital laboratories provide a wide range of blood tests to diagnose and monitor various health conditions. These tests help doctors assess organ function, detect infections, and identify diseases such as diabetes, anemia, and high cholesterol. Common blood tests include complete blood count (CBC), blood glucose tests, liver and kidney function tests, and lipid profiles.

Blood samples are collected by trained professionals and analyzed using advanced medical equipment to ensure accurate and timely results. Regular blood testing plays a crucial role in preventive healthcare, enabling early detection and effective management of illnesses.

Radiology Support

Radiology support is an essential part of hospital laboratory services, providing imaging techniques to assist in diagnosing and treating medical conditions. This includes X-rays, ultrasounds, and MRI scans, which help doctors visualize internal structures such as bones, organs, and tissues. Radiology plays a critical role in detecting fractures, tumors, infections, and internal injuries.

Advanced imaging technologies ensure high-resolution images, improving diagnostic accuracy and guiding treatment plans. Many hospitals integrate radiology with lab services to provide a comprehensive approach to patient care.

CT scan

A Computed Tomography (CT) scan is a specialized imaging procedure that combines multiple X-ray images to create detailed cross-sectional views of the body. CT scans are commonly used to detect tumors, internal injuries, infections, and abnormalities in organs such as the brain, lungs, and abdomen. Hospitals utilize modern CT scanning technology to provide fast and precise results, aiding in emergency and routine medical care. This non-invasive procedure helps doctors diagnose conditions more accurately and plan appropriate treatments for patients.

Pregnancery Tests

Hospital labs offer pregnancy tests to confirm whether a woman is expecting. These tests detect the presence of human chorionic gonadotropin (hCG), a hormone produced during pregnancy, either in urine or blood samples. Blood tests for pregnancy provide more precise results and can detect pregnancy earlier than home test kits. Additionally, hospitals may offer further evaluations such as ultrasound scans to monitor fetal development.
